Yankees Sign Star Pitcher

By Sports Desk

The New York Yankees made a blockbuster move yesterday, signing All-Star pitcher Marcus Rodriguez to a five-year, $125 million contract. The deal, announced at a packed press conference at Yankee Stadium, signals the franchise's commitment to reclaiming championship glory.

"This is a game-changer for us," said Yankees General Manager Brian Cashman. "Marcus brings elite talent, leadership, and a winning mentality that will elevate our entire pitching staff."

Rodriguez, 28, posted a 2.45 ERA last season with 245 strikeouts. The three-time All-Star expressed excitement about joining the storied franchise. "Growing up, I dreamed of wearing pinstripes," Rodriguez said. "This is where legends are made, and I'm ready to contribute to that legacy."

The signing addresses the Yankees' most pressing need heading into spring training. Fans have already begun lining up for tickets, with season pass renewals up 30% since the announcement.
