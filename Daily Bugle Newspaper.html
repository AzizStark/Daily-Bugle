Wall-Crawler Strikes Again

By J. Jonah Jameson, Editor-in-Chief

Once again, the menace known as Spider-Man has taken the law into his own hands. Despite repeated warnings from this publication and law enforcement officials, the masked vigilante continues to operate with impunity throughout our fair city.

Yesterday's incident near the Brooklyn Bridge left three vehicles damaged and traffic backed up for miles. While some witnesses claim Spider-Man was "helping," this reporter knows better. Where there's smoke, there's fire, and where there's Spider-Man, there's trouble.

The NYPD has yet to comment on their continued inability to apprehend this wall-crawling criminal. Citizens deserve better protection from costumed menaces who think they're above the law.

By Ben Urich, Senior Reporter

Mayor Wilson announced a comprehensive new initiative to combat rising crime rates in Manhattan. The plan includes increased police presence in high-crime areas and new technology to track criminal activity.

"We will not let our city be held hostage by criminals, whether they wear masks or not," the Mayor stated at yesterday's press conference. The initiative has received mixed reactions from city council members.
