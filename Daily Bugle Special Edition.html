Oscorp Explosion Rocks Manhattan!

Dozens Injured as Research Lab Destroyed in Mysterious Blast

Smoke billows from Oscorp Tower following last night's explosion. Photo by Eddie Brock.

Disaster Strikes at Midnight

By J. Jonah Jameson & Ben Urich

A massive explosion ripped through Oscorp Industries' research facility late last night, sending shockwaves across Manhattan and leaving at least 47 people injured. The blast, which occurred at approximately 11:47 PM, destroyed three floors of the company's headquarters and could be felt as far away as Brooklyn.

Emergency services rushed to the scene as flames engulfed the 45th through 47th floors of the iconic Oscorp Tower. Firefighters battled the blaze for over four hours before bringing it under control. The building has been evacuated and declared structurally unstable.

"It sounded like thunder," said nearby resident Maria Gonzalez. "My windows shattered, and I saw this huge fireball shooting out of the building. I've never seen anything like it."

FDNY Commissioner Thomas Riley confirmed that 47 people were transported to area hospitals with injuries ranging from minor cuts to severe burns. Miraculously, no fatalities have been reported, though three individuals remain in critical condition at New York Presbyterian Hospital.

Questions Mount Over Cause

By Frederick Foswell

While officials have not yet determined the cause of the explosion, sources close to the investigation suggest it originated in Oscorp's controversial genetic research division. The company has faced scrutiny in recent months over safety protocols in its experimental laboratories.

CEO Norman Osborn, who was not in the building at the time of the blast, released a statement early this morning: "We are devastated by this tragedy. Our thoughts are with the injured and their families. We are cooperating fully with investigators to determine what happened."

However, critics are demanding answers. City Councilwoman Patricia Hayes has called for an immediate investigation into Oscorp's safety record. "This company has been operating with minimal oversight for too long," Hayes stated. "The people of New York deserve to know what dangerous experiments were being conducted in the heart of our city."

Spider-Man on Scene

By Eddie Brock

Multiple witnesses report that the masked vigilante known as Spider-Man was among the first responders at the scene, pulling victims from the burning building before firefighters arrived. While some hail his actions as heroic, others question what he might have been doing at Oscorp at that hour.

"He was swinging people down from the upper floors," said witness David Park. "Without him, I don't think everyone would have made it out."

City Responds to Crisis

By Betty Brant

Mayor Wilson declared a state of emergency for the affected area and activated the city's disaster response protocols. Several nearby buildings remain evacuated as structural engineers assess potential damage from the blast.

"The safety of New Yorkers is our top priority," the Mayor said at a 4 AM press conference. "We will get to the bottom of what happened here."

Public transportation has been disrupted, with the nearby subway station closed indefinitely. Commuters are advised to seek alternate routes.

Oscorp's Troubled History

By Robbie Robertson

This is not the first incident at Oscorp facilities. In 2019, a chemical spill resulted in a temporary shutdown of operations. Last year, three employees filed safety complaints with OSHA, though the company denied any wrongdoing.

Former employee Dr. Curtis Connors, who left the company in 2023, has been vocal about what he calls "reckless experimentation" at Oscorp. "I warned them something like this could happen," Connors said in a phone interview this morning.

What Happens Next?

By Ned Leeds

Federal investigators from the ATF and FBI are expected to arrive today to join local authorities in determining the cause of the explosion. The investigation could take weeks or even months.

Oscorp's stock plummeted 35% in pre-market trading, and analysts predict the company faces billions in liability claims. Several law firms have already announced class-action lawsuits on behalf of victims.
