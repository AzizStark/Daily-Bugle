METRO NEWS

Mysterious Lights Over Queens

By Frederick Foswell, Metro Reporter

Residents of Queens reported seeing unusual lights in the sky last night around 11:30 PM. The phenomenon, described as "bright blue streaks" by witnesses, lasted approximately fifteen minutes before disappearing without a trace.

"I was taking out the trash when I saw them," said local resident Maria Gonzalez. "At first I thought it was fireworks, but they moved in patterns I've never seen before. It was both beautiful and terrifying."

The NYPD has received over 200 calls about the incident. Officials from the FAA confirmed no aircraft were in the area at the time. Dr. Curt Connors from Empire State University suggests the lights could be atmospheric phenomena related to recent solar activity.

OPINION & EDITORIAL

Editor's Note: Accountability Matters

By J. Jonah Jameson

For too long, this city has turned a blind eye to vigilantes operating outside the law. While some may call them heroes, I call them a threat to the very foundation of our justice system.

When masked individuals take the law into their own hands, they undermine the police, the courts, and every institution we've built to maintain order. Today it's stopping a robbery. Tomorrow? Who decides what's right and wrong?

We have laws for a reason. We have due process for a reason. And we have elected officials and trained law enforcement for a reason. No one—no matter how powerful or well-intentioned—should be above accountability.

The Daily Bugle will continue to ask the tough questions and demand transparency, even when it's unpopular. That's our duty to you, the citizens of New York.
