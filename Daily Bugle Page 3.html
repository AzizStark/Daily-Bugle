Alligator Found in Central Park!

By Gloria Grant, Lifestyle Editor

In what officials are calling "the most New York thing ever," a four-foot alligator was discovered sunbathing near the Turtle Pond in Central Park yesterday afternoon. The reptile, which joggers initially mistook for a large piece of driftwood, caused quite a stir when it yawned.

"I was just doing my morning run when I saw what I thought was a log," said witness Marcus Thompson. "Then it MOVED. I've never run faster in my life!"

Park Rangers successfully captured the alligator using chicken drumsticks and a net. The reptile, affectionately dubbed "Gator Jameson" by social media users (in reference to our esteemed editor's tenacity), is believed to be a former pet that was illegally released.

"This is actually more common than people think," explained Wildlife Expert Dr. Sarah Martinez. "People buy baby alligators, realize they grow quite large, and then make poor decisions."

Gator Jameson is now residing at the Bronx Zoo and is reportedly doing well, enjoying a diet of fish and basking under heat lamps. The zoo is considering making him an educational ambassador about the dangers of exotic pet ownership.

Editor's Note

J. Jonah Jameson is NOT amused by the "Gator Jameson" nickname. The alligator shows more restraint than certain masked vigilantes, we'll give him that.
